Overview

The NHM Tours app for mobile presents storylines through museum exhibits, engaging visitors in a new way. NHM Tours has an educational mission, providing additional interactive dimensions to museum experiences.

The app is designed for museum visitors, guiding them through digital tours step-by-step. Users can choose between a number of digital tours, navigate the museum map and scan QR codes of exhibit items of interest.

The main objectives of the NHM Tours Project included finding solutions how to present information in an interesting and captivating way, how to guide users through the museum towards specific exhibits and to create a barrier-free museum experience.

My Role

This case study allowed me to dive into the specifics for designing for iOS and for Android products. I also took the opportunity to gain some experience designing in dark mode. Within this project I also practiced my skills in animating with Adobe XD to improve the quality of my prototypes.

UX Design

Competitor analysis, user flows, understanding storyline content, wireframing (low, mid), understanding and application of Human Interface Guidelines and Material Design guidelines.

UI Design

Wireframing (mid, high, visual design, sound and physical feedback design, gestures & interactions, prototyping animations, user testing, mockups).

User flows

  • Users want to find appealing virtual tours for their museum visit
  • Users want to have an overview of the tours and have the option to skip ahead
  • Users want to be able to interact with exhibits in an engaging way
  • Users want to find more Information on exhibit pieces which interest them
  • Users want to have a quick orientation through the museum exhibit halls

Background Research

In order to design a comprehensive and engaging tour, research was done on the material to be featured, including the museum layout, the exhibits and the storyline of the tour.

Mobile Gestures (iPhone 14 Pro)

Home (tours)

On the home page you can swipe through the available tours to the left and right in a carousel.

Overview page

On the overview tour screen you can tap the start button to start the tour from the beginning, or the individual exhibit images to navigate to the specific exhibit page. By swiping up and down you can scroll through the list of exhibit items.

Exhibit tour

On the exhibit page you can swipe up and down to scroll through the page. Several buttons can be tapped to activate them, such as the audio controls and the transcript button. You can swipe left and right to move the audio marker along the track to rewind or fast forward.

Prototype testing

3 user tests for iOS, 2 user tests for Android

  • Spacing of map titles has been improved (iOS)
  • Card appearance was adapted for maps (iOS & Android)
  • Duplicate texts have been removed (Android)
  • Checkmarks turn to x when clicked (Android)
  • A logo was added in the Header to link back to the start page (iOS & Android)
  • "Tours" has been renamed to "Home" in the hamburger menu (Android)
  • The button "Guide" has been renamed "Map Guide" (iOS & Android)
  • "Featured" has been renamed to "Featured tour" (iOS & Android)
  • The tops of cards were made visible to indicate scrolling (iOS & Android)
